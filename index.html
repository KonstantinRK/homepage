Hi! ^_^

I am a PhD Student under the supervision of Thomas A. Henzinger at the Institute of Science and Technology (IST Austria). In my research, I use tools from non-asymptotic statistics to develop monitors for detecting undesirable behaviour in stochastic systems. In particular, I am interested in making systems with learned components more responsible, e.g. fair and private.

Before affiliating with my current research group I was fortunate enough to work on projects with Christoph Lampert ("Out-of-specification testing of convolutional neural networks during runtime") and Krishnendu Chatterjee ("Dynamic data race detection in TSO-model").

Before starting my PhD, I completed my M.Sc. in "Logic and Computation" at the Vienna University of Technology under the supervision of Agata Ciabattoni and my B.Sc. in "Business" and "Business Informatics" at the Vienna University of Economics and Business. While completing my masters I was a research assistant under Mark Strembeck working on temporal networks.

Throughout the years I held several teaching assistant positions for courses such as: Data Science, Networks, Network Security, Knowledge Based Systems, Formal Methods, and Formalisms Every Computer Scientist Should Know.

My research interests include: Runtime Verification, Formal Methods, Stochastic Processes, Non-Asymptotic Statistics, Time Series, Learning Theory, Responsible Computing.
